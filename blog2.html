Minimally Invasive Spine Surgery: What You Need To Know

14th June, 2024 by Experts

Minimally invasive spine surgery (MISS) has revolutionized the treatment of various spinal conditions, offering patients a safer and quicker recovery option compared to traditional open surgeries.

Key Features of Minimally Invasive Spine Surgery

Unlike traditional open surgery, MISS involves smaller incisions, typically less than an inch long, through which specialized surgical instruments and a camera (endoscope) are inserted. This allows surgeons to perform complex procedures with:

  • Reduced damage to surrounding muscles and tissues
  • Less blood loss during the surgery
  • Lower risk of infection
  • Faster recovery times and shorter hospital stays

Conditions Treated with MISS

Minimally invasive techniques are effective for treating a variety of spinal conditions, including:

  • Herniated discs
  • Spinal stenosis
  • Spinal tumors
  • Vertebral fractures
  • Spinal deformities (such as scoliosis)

Benefits to Patients

Patient benefits of minimally invasive spine surgery include:

  • Quicker recovery and return to daily activities
  • Less post-operative pain and discomfort
  • Minimal scarring and improved cosmetic outcomes
  • Reduced risk of complications such as infections
